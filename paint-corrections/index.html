What Is Paint Correction?

Precision Paint Restoration

Paint correction is the art of removing surface imperfections to restore your vehicle's original clarity, depth, and gloss. It's not a wax or a polish — it's a multi-step refinement process.

Swirl Marks

Fine circular scratches from improper washing or drying. Our correction removes them completely for a mirror-like finish.

Deep Scratches

Key marks, brush scratches, and surface scuffs — we carefully level the clear coat to diminish or erase them.

Oxidation

UV and environmental damage that dulls paint. We restore the original color depth and gloss with precision compounding.

Water Spots

Hard water minerals etched into the clear coat. Our multi-stage process safely removes them without damaging the paint.

i

Every correction begins with a thorough inspection and paint-depth measurement. We use professional-grade Rupes polishers, CarPro compounds, and microfiber technology to ensure zero haze or micro-marring.

Correction Levels

Single-Stage vs Multi-Stage

Not every vehicle needs the same level of correction. We assess your paint condition and recommend the right approach — from a light enhancement to a full concours-level restoration.

Stage 1

Single-Stage Correction

A one-pass polish refinement that removes light swirl marks, minor oxidation, and superficial water spots. Ideal for paint in good condition that just needs a deep refresh and enhanced gloss.

  • ✓ Removes 70-85% of light swirls & haze
  • ✓ Single polishing pass with fine compound
  • ✓ Adds deep wet-look gloss
  • ✓ Best for maintained vehicles under 3 years old
Typical time: 3–5 hours $450+
Stage 2+

Multi-Stage Correction

A comprehensive 2–4 step process using progressive compounds and polishes to remove deep scratches, heavy oxidation, etched water spots, and severe swirls. Delivers a show-car, concours-level finish.

  • ✓ Removes 90-98% of imperfections
  • ✓ Heavy compounding + medium polish + fine refinement
  • ✓ Eliminates deep scratches & etched water spots
  • ✓ Best for neglected paint or high-end show vehicles
Typical time: 8–16 hours $850+

Every vehicle is unique. We provide free in-person estimates to assess your paint and recommend the right stage.
